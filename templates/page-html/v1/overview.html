Module 1

Introduction to Human Factors & Signal Detection Theory

PSY 562: Advanced Human Factors  ·  Spring A 2026  ·  Jan 12–18

Why this module matters: Before you can improve forensic practice, you need a clear-eyed model of how human performance actually works — and how it fails. This module gives you that foundation. Signal Detection Theory (SDT) in particular is a lens you'll use throughout the entire course to analyze trade-offs between sensitivity and specificity in forensic judgment.

Module Introduction

Welcome to PSY 562. In this first module, you'll build the conceptual vocabulary that underlies everything else in the course. Human factors is the study of how people interact with systems, tools, and environments — and forensic science is full of high-stakes moments where human cognitive limits directly affect outcomes.

We start with Signal Detection Theory, a framework developed in the 1950s that remains one of the most powerful tools for analyzing decisions made under uncertainty. You'll see how forensic examiners — like any decision-maker — are always navigating a tension between sensitivity (catching real signals) and specificity (avoiding false alarms). Understanding that tension is step one in designing systems and standards that work with human cognition rather than against it.

Topics Covered

What Is Human Factors?

The science of optimizing human performance in complex systems — and understanding why errors are often systemic, not individual failures.

Human Performance and Error

Taxonomies of human error (slips, lapses, mistakes), the role of expertise and context, and why well-trained people still make consequential errors.

Cognitive & Emotional Biases in Forensic Work

Confirmation bias, contextual bias, and emotional influences that shape forensic examination — and the evidence for how widespread they are.

Signal Detection Theory (SDT)

The mathematical framework for analyzing hits, misses, false alarms, and correct rejections — and how decision criteria affect accuracy outcomes.

Interpreting Forensic Standards

How professional standards (OSAC, FBI, ISO) encode human factors principles — and how to read them with a human performance lens.

Linking HF to Forensic Practice

Connecting theory to the real-world contexts you work in — DNA analysis, latent prints, digital forensics, crime scene investigation, and more.
